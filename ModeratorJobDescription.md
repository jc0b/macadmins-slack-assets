# Mac Admins Slack moderator job description

Moderators are the people that community members will go to for help when they need or want direction or guidance in the community.

### **Volunteering**

* This is a volunteer role, but we ask that you commit to being available during your (work) day to respond to community concerns and act on them with regularity. There’s an expectation that you may occasionally spend 1-2 hours involved in admin discussions about issues.  
* If you can provide an *approximate* time of day you prefer to be online to help (mornings, midday, afternoon, evening) and timezone, this will help us with planning. Don’t worry, we realize schedules change and vacations happen; our goal is to create a general availability document for the moderators.

### **Responsibilities**

**Essential:**

* Be available during the day (within your timezone and general availability) to respond when moderators are paged, especially for time-sensitive matters (i.e., accidental post of passwords, tokens, etc.).  
* Review discussions and posts for adherence to our **Code of Conduct** when requested / alerted to issues. If posts or responses don’t comply, address or remove them, inform the individuals and suggest ways to comply going forward.  
* Use established systems to track and complete requests (examples: remove threads, update or delete accounts); create and maintain moderator documentation for processes, guidelines, actions taken, and so on.  
* Communicate feedback, concerns, and ideas to our team of moderators.  
* Participate in periodic reviews of **Code of Conduct** and other governance practices, along with the rest of the moderator team *(approval process is still tbd, but developing & following that process is included)*.

**Optional, depending on time and preference:**

* Encourage community participation and engagement.  
* React to or comment on posts with valuable insights.

### **Benefits**

* Contribute to strengthening the Mac Admins community.  
* Gain experience and expand your network within the Mac Admins sphere.  
* Learn the art of community moderation with support from experienced mentors.  
* Build skills in governance and policy enforcement.  
* Communicate directly with the MAF to shape the future of our Slack instance.  
* Be recognized as a trusted leader in our community.  
* Gain volunteer experience for your resume or Linkedin profile.

### **Skills**

**Required:**

* Strong English language skills (reading and writing).  
* Proficiency in using Slack and digital communication tools, along with Google Workspace for collaboration.  
* Conflict management abilities to handle heated discussions calmly and objectively while maintaining professionalism.  
* The capability to work independently while effectively communicating within a small team.  
* No major “strikes” or previous conflicts in the community that would create difficulties working with any of the moderators or Board members.  
* Participate in training including processes for Slack moderation, moderation tools, and an online moderator training course via asynchronous training (\~2.5 hours).  
* Acknowledge and agree to the MAF’s volunteer legal policies. (add link when ready)  

**Good to Have:**

* Open-mindedness and adaptability.  
* A desire to embrace opportunities to learn and grow new skills.  
* Broad understanding of managing and administering Macs (not required, but it’ll help a lot).  
* Familiarity with and prior participation in the community.  
* If you have fluency in other languages, please let us know.

### **Applying community rules and guidelines**

The most familiar part of a moderator’s role is applying community rules and guidelines when necessary. This ensures that members are encouraged and able to participate freely, topics are discussed in appropriate channels, and the community remains a place where members want to participate.

Use discretion when deciding how and when to deal with a violation. A moderator’s purpose is to keep the community safe and running smoothly, not simply to wield power. Things to consider might be how new to the community the member is, their past behavior (do they have a bad track record?), whether the behavior in question is affecting other community members or the health of the overall community, or whether allowing it to continue will set a negative precedent.

If a member violates the rules or guidelines, there are several options at a moderator’s disposal. It's important to give a sense of due process by starting with the lightest appropriate sanction, and if necessary, to escalate methodically.

If a sanction is imposed, record it in accordance with the defined community procedures. *\[To be shared and noted during training.\]*

It is the role of the moderator to ensure that no inappropriate content is posted as per the community guidelines. This may include (but is not limited to) pornography or adult content, pirated or illegal content, and self­-promotional or affiliate links. Remove any inappropriate content by deleting it in accordance with the processes of our community. In general, moderators are alerted when something questionable is posted, but they have the ability to proactively remove if they see it before being alerted. (It just needs to be documented following usual protocol.)

### **Dealing with Member Complaints**

When a member makes a complaint about another member of the community it is a moderator’s responsibility to handle the complaint in a professional manner. This is for their own protection, as well as to protect the nature of the community.

Complaints may be petty, abusive, exaggerated, or in some cases, completely false. Regardless of these above factors, it is important to resist the temptation to argue, or refute the complaint. If a moderator doesn’t feel that they can remain neutral, or the complaint falls into a “gray area” where the moderator doesn’t feel comfortable making a decision, the complaint should be escalated to another moderator or discussed within the moderator group for consensus.

A moderator who responds negatively to a complaint (even if it’s just with an emoji) may have their actions reviewed by the moderator team or board if it’s determined that their comments are detrimental to the situation. Moderators should never participate in making conflicts more heated.

Any complaint process should begin with getting all the facts, and by making it clear that the complaint has been heard. This affords the complainant time to calm down if they are upset. A realistic (achievable) timeline for following up should be offered, and then the facts investigated. If the complaint is serious or urgent (i.e. if someone’s personal information has been shared), take immediate action.

When the evidence has been collected, consider all possible resolutions, as well as any unintended outcomes. Sometimes a resolution may need to be discussed amongst a team. Make a decision, and acknowledge any errors made, if necessary. Inform the complainant of the outcome. Follow up with any other parties involved. Most importantly, document, document, document following the moderator process currently in place.

When dealing with a complaint about the Mac Admins Foundation, it is important to reach out to a Board member, either via shared channel or DM, and let them respond. If the complaint is about a specific board member, it is acceptable to DM a different board member to report the complaint. It’s important to acknowledge receipt and communicate that escalation process. 

### **Resolving Conflicts**

It is natural for conflicts to occur from time to time, and it is the role of the moderator to monitor the situation and step in if necessary. Don’t jump in right away the moment a topic gets a little heated, as sensitive topics often engage people, but keep a very close eye on things, because the absence of visual and auditory cues, in combination with invisibility, anonymity, and delayed reactions, can cause online conflict to escalate fast. A moderator should intervene if a member resorts to personally insulting another member, using language that breaches the community guidelines, or posts anything that a reasonable person would consider offensive, abusive, or hate speech. There are a few channels in particular that tend to have more conflict than others and may need more close monitoring than others.

As a first course of action, a moderator should try posting within the thread to get things back on track. If the conversation continues to escalate, then warnings, strikes, and even removal may need to occur.

### **Structuring the Community**

In the near future, we’ll be looking at ways to better structure the community by consolidating and archiving the large number of channels we currently have. To that end, we may ask moderators for input on future structure. 

In channels where first-time members may participate for the first time, moderators are encouraged to welcome them and help them find the best place to ask their questions. It is also the role of the moderator to help gently guide discussions away from conflict when responses (to new members in particular) may discourage participation or drive away new members. This is about encouraging a welcoming culture of kindness no matter the level of knowledge a member comes to us with.
